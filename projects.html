Project: Feed A Friend

Course: AP CSA (AP Computer Science A)

Duration: May 3-24, 2019

Objective: develop and market an idea/product to judges

Type of project: partner

An enormous amount of food is wasted when many people are still hungry - over ⅓ of all food produced globally goes to waste. At the same time, millions of Americans go to bed hungry without meals, and numerous citizens constantly live below the poverty line. These two problems seemed intuitive for me and my teammate to tackle, and thus Feed A Friend was born. We created a prototype for a mobile application where restaurants, grocery stores, or any other certified food companies can give their leftovers to someone who needs it more. Personal users can create a consumer account where they can buy a meal for a significantly reduced price or even free. Companies that wish to sell their leftover food can create business accounts where they can add an offer for meals whenever they have extras. My role was primarily involved with the UI/UX side of the project as well as the marketing, outreach, and documentation aspects. The project came to a close with my well-received presentation, leading us to place in the top three projects of our class.


Project: What 2 Watch

Course: AP CSA (AP Computer Science A)

Duration: February 11-April 11, 2019

Objective: design and develop an Android application that solved an issue

Type of project: partner

As Netflix, Hulu, and other streaming platforms grow in popularity, encouraging “binge-watching” with an overwhelming selection of media, it can be difficult to know which shows would best suit the interests of an individual. What 2 Watch was developed to combat this issue by providing a simple user interface that would filter through a list of movies and TV shows from IMDB based on the genres that the user selects, and then displays the resulting list with selections that match these preferences. During this project, I took a leadership role in developing the app in Android Studio. I learned developing skills including usage of Fragments, ArrayListAdapters, Enums, and more. Over halfway through the project, my partner and I presented a status report of What 2 Watch, where we received positive and constructive feedback and gauged a better understanding of our next steps in making our app more functional. At the end of the project, we presented our functional app via gallery walk. The source code for the application is available on GitHub.


Project: Liberate (Entrepreneurship Project 2018)

Course: POE (Principles of Engineering)

Duration: May 4-25, 2018

Objective: develop and market an idea/product to judges

Type of project: group (position: team leader)

Liberate was a nonprofit e-commerce website whose proceeds from the website would go toward slave liberation organizations, such as Free the Slaves, Not for Sale, and International Justice Mission in efforts to end modern-day slavery. The experience was unique in that it exclusively featured fair trade certified goods, so consumers knew that the products they were receiving were not made at the expense of human freedom. During the duration of this project, I designed and coded most of the front-end. (If the deadline was extended, I would work on backend as well, but time constraints did not allow this part to be actualized.) My role also extended into documentation of the project, as I wrote the problem and solution statements, research summary, and more.


Project: Solar Hydrogen Vehicle

Course: POE (Principles of Engineering)

Duration: November 1, 2017 - November 17, 2017

Objective: investigate and prepare a statement regarding the most efficient mode of alternative energy

Type of project: group (team leader)

Our team created a solar-hydrogen vehicle that could travel one meter using four different energy configurations: 1 solar cell, 1 hydrogen cell, 2 hydrogen cells, or 2 solar cells. Using these instructions and guidelines and this rubric, we concluded that the more efficient form of energy was using 2 solar cells. As team leader, I supervised and contributed to the building process and kept the project report updated, ensuring that our group was staying on track as per the Gantt chart.


Project: Dashable (Entrepreneurship Project 2017)

Course: CSE (Computer Science/Software Engineering)

Duration: April 2017 - June 2017

Objective: develop and market an idea/product to judges

Type of project: group

Dashable was created to make safety easily accessible by just a press of a button. My team and I developed a modification to the Amazon Dash button where the user could pre-arrange an emergency phone call message or text to send whenever the button was pressed. This way, if one ever found themselves in a situation in which they did not feel safe and needed a discreet way to send for help, they had a way to do so. I developed an Android app where the user could input a message that would send when the button was pressed using UDP packets. Another role of mine was the documentation of the project, which I single-handedly completed. Our website was a work-in-progress online store in which people could purchase the product.
